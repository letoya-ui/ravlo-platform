Compensation Overview

Your production drives your income.

As a Loan Officer, your compensation is tied to performance, deal generation, borrower management, and successful loan closings. This page provides a general overview of how commission works inside the Caughman Mason and Ravlo ecosystem.

Compensation Model Commission-based and performance-driven
Primary Driver Closed and funded loan production

How Commission Works

A simple overview of how earnings are typically generated.

Lead Generation Loan Officers are expected to bring in and work opportunities that fit the company’s lending and real estate goals.
Borrower Guidance Compensation is tied not just to generating a lead, but helping move the borrower and file toward completion.
Closed Production Commission is generally earned when a deal successfully closes and/or funds, based on the agreed structure.
Performance Alignment Your earning potential increases as your consistency, quality, and production increase.

What Impacts Earnings

The main factors that influence commission outcomes.

01

Deal Volume

The number of qualified deals you bring in and actively manage.

02

Deal Quality

Well-structured deals with responsive borrowers move more efficiently and improve outcomes.

03

Follow-Through

Strong communication and file management help improve closing rates.

04

Closed Results

Final earnings are tied to successfully completed production under the company’s compensation structure.

Important Notes

General expectations regarding commission and compensation.

Commission structure may vary by role type, deal type, and agreement.
No commission should be assumed outside of the formal compensation agreement.
Payout timing may depend on closing, funding, and company processing procedures.
Team support, split structures, or referral structures may apply depending on the file.

What Success Looks Like

The habits that tend to lead to stronger earnings over time.

Consistent lead generation
Professional borrower communication
Fast follow-up and file movement
Reliable closings and funded production
