\# Visual OS Asset Generation



Visual OS Asset Generation defines how Ravlo’s cinematic visuals are created — hero frames, floating panels, blueprint backgrounds, and OS compositions. These assets bring the Ravlo universe to life across product, marketing, and documentation.



\## Purpose



Asset Generation answers:

“How do we create visuals that feel unmistakably Ravlo?”



It provides:

\- hero frame templates  

\- panel composition rules  

\- background generation rules  

\- lighting setups  

\- blueprint overlays  

\- naming conventions  

\- export standards  



\## Asset Categories



1\. Hero OS Frames  

2\. Module Frames  

3\. Floating Panel Compositions  

4\. Blueprint Backgrounds  

5\. Dashboard Frames  

6\. Detail View Frames  

7\. Ecosystem Frames  

8\. Node Ring Frames  



Each category has its own structure and rules.



\---



\# 1. Hero OS Frames



Hero OS Frames are the cinematic, wide‑format visuals used for:

\- homepage hero  

\- product reveal  

\- press kit  

\- investor decks  

\- module introductions  



\## Composition



\- Wide 16:9 or 21:9 aspect ratio  

\- Central OS panel cluster  

\- Soft spotlight from above  

\- Blueprint ring behind panels  

\- Subtle atmospheric haze  



\## Layers



\- Background (midnight gradient)  

\- Blueprint ring  

\- Floating OS panels  

\- Glow accents  

\- Title or label (optional)  



\## Naming



`ravlo-hero-os-v1.png`



\---



\# 2. Module Frames



Each module gets its own cinematic frame:

\- Command Center  

\- Deal Architect  

\- Build Studio  

\- Rehab Studio  

\- Capital Studio  

\- Portfolio Intelligence  

\- Partner Marketplace  



\## Composition



\- One primary panel  

\- Two to four supporting panels  

\- Module‑specific blueprint accents  

\- Soft rim light  



\## Naming



`ravlo-\[module]-frame-v1.png`



\---



\# 3. Floating Panel Compositions



Floating panels are used for:

\- product pages  

\- documentation  

\- marketing visuals  



\## Rules



\- Panels must float with 12–24px depth  

\- Soft inner shadow  

\- Subtle outer glow  

\- Blueprint lines behind panels  



\## Naming



`ravlo-panel-stack-\[module]-v1.png`



\---



\# 4. Blueprint Backgrounds



Blueprint backgrounds create the architectural identity of Ravlo.



\## Types



\- Blueprint grid  

\- Node rings  

\- Technical corner marks  

\- Soft gradient overlays  



\## Rules



\- Blueprint lines must be subtle  

\- Never overpower the UI  

\- Use 8px grid alignment  



\## Naming



`ravlo-blueprint-bg-v1.png`

\---



\# Export Standards



\## Formats

\- PNG (primary)  

\- WebP (web optimized)  

\- SVG (blueprint elements only)  



\## Sizes

\- 1920×1080 (standard)  

\- 2560×1440 (hi‑res)  

\- 3840×2160 (4K)  



\## Color Profile

\- sRGB  

\- 300 DPI for print  



\---



\# Do



\- Keep visuals cinematic and premium.  

\- Use lighting to guide attention.  

\- Maintain consistent depth and spacing.  

\- Keep blueprint elements subtle.  



\# Don’t



\- Overuse glow or neon effects.  

\- Flatten panels into generic SaaS visuals.  

\- Break the circular ecosystem structure.
