Flow Patterns



Flow patterns define the reusable structures behind Ravlo’s guided, step‑based experiences. They help users move through complex tasks with clarity, confidence, and a sense of calm progression.



Ravlo flow patterns are designed to feel:

\- guided

\- structured

\- predictable

\- calm

\- cinematic



1\. Purpose

Flow patterns break complex processes into clear, manageable steps. They reduce cognitive load, prevent overwhelm, and help investors and partners complete tasks with accuracy and confidence.



Flow patterns are used for:

\- deal analysis workflows

\- renovation and build planning

\- capital requests and underwriting

\- onboarding and verification

\- document workflows

\- multi‑step applications

\- partner intake and approvals



2\. Types of Flow Patterns

Ravlo uses a disciplined set of flow pattern structures:



Linear Step Flow

A simple, forward‑moving sequence of steps with clear progress indicators.



Segmented Flow

A flow divided into major sections, each containing multiple steps.



Panel‑Based Flow

A layout where each step appears inside a structured panel for clarity and hierarchy.



Wizard Flow

A guided, immersive flow with one step visible at a time.



Review Flow

A final step that displays all entered data for confirmation before submission.



Branching Flow

A conditional flow where steps change based on user input or scenario.



3\. Anatomy

A Ravlo flow pattern includes:

\- step indicator or progress bar

\- clear step title and description

\- structured content region

\- primary and secondary actions

\- optional sidebar or summary panel

\- spacing and alignment based on tokens

\- responsive behavior across breakpoints



4\. Behavior

Flow patterns must:

\- guide users without overwhelming them

\- maintain consistent spacing and rhythm

\- clearly communicate progress and next steps

\- prevent accidental loss of data

\- support keyboard navigation and accessibility

\- reinforce Ravlo’s calm, cinematic presentation



5\. Design Principles

Flow patterns follow Ravlo’s core principles:

\- Blueprint alignment: disciplined structure and step hierarchy

\- Cinematic spacing: generous breathing room between steps

\- Clarity over cleverness: simple, predictable progression

\- Emotional safety: calm, guided movement through complexity



Flow patterns are the backbone of Ravlo’s guided experiences. They turn complex workflows into clear, confident journeys.
