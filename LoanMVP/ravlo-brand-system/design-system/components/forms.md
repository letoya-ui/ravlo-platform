Forms



Forms are one of the most critical components in the Ravlo ecosystem. They collect information, guide workflows, and help investors complete tasks with clarity and confidence.



Ravlo forms are designed to feel:

\- structured

\- calm

\- predictable

\- investor‑grade

\- cinematic



1\. Purpose

Forms gather data in a way that reduces friction and supports decision‑making. They should never feel overwhelming or chaotic.



Forms are used for:

\- deal analysis inputs

\- property details

\- borrower information

\- partner onboarding

\- settings and preferences

\- workflow steps

\- uploads and submissions



2\. Types of Forms

Ravlo uses a disciplined set of form structures:



Simple Form

A short form with a few fields, used for quick inputs.



Sectioned Form

A form divided into labeled sections for clarity and organization.



Multi‑Step Form

A guided flow that breaks complex tasks into manageable steps.



Inline Form

A lightweight form used inside cards or panels for quick edits.



Review Form

A form that displays entered data for confirmation before submission.



3\. Anatomy

A Ravlo form includes:

\- clear labels

\- input fields

\- spacing based on tokens

\- section headers (optional)

\- helper text (optional)

\- validation messages

\- primary and secondary actions



4\. Behavior

Forms must:

\- maintain consistent spacing and alignment

\- avoid overcrowding fields

\- use clear, descriptive labels

\- provide real‑time validation when appropriate

\- support keyboard navigation and screen readers

\- scale gracefully on mobile

\- avoid unnecessary required fields



5\. Design Principles

Forms follow Ravlo’s core principles:

\- Blueprint alignment: strong grid structure

\- Cinematic spacing: generous breathing room

\- Clarity over cleverness: simple, direct inputs

\- Emotional safety: calm, predictable interactions



Forms are the engine of data collection in the Ravlo OS. They transform complexity into clarity and help investors move forward with confidence.
