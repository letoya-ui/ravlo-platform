Cards



Cards are one of the core structural elements in the Ravlo interface. They group related information into clear, modular containers that create rhythm, hierarchy, and focus across dashboards and OS modules.



Ravlo cards are designed to feel:

\- structured

\- premium

\- spacious

\- intentional

\- cinematic



1\. Purpose

Cards organize content into digestible sections. They help users scan, compare, and understand information without feeling overwhelmed.



Cards are used for:

\- metrics

\- summaries

\- snapshots

\- lists

\- previews

\- actions

\- data groupings



2\. Types of Cards

Ravlo uses a disciplined set of card types:



Standard Card

A flexible container for general content. Used across dashboards and modules.



Metric Card

A compact card focused on a single key number or insight.



Panel Card

A larger card used for multi‑section content, forms, or workflows.



Action Card

A card that highlights a primary action or next step.



Snapshot Card

A card that shows the latest activity or most recent item from a module.



3\. Anatomy

A Ravlo card includes:

\- container surface

\- header (optional)

\- body content

\- footer or actions (optional)

\- spacing based on tokens

\- radius based on radii tokens

\- shadow or glow based on elevation tokens



4\. Behavior

Cards must:

\- maintain consistent padding

\- align to the grid

\- support responsive resizing

\- avoid overcrowding

\- use clear hierarchy between header, content, and actions



5\. Design Principles

Cards follow Ravlo’s core principles:

\- Blueprint alignment: clean grid structure

\- Cinematic spacing: generous breathing room

\- Clarity over cleverness: simple, readable content

\- Emotional safety: calm, stable surfaces



Cards are the backbone of Ravlo’s visual rhythm. They create structure, clarity, and confidence across the entire platform.
