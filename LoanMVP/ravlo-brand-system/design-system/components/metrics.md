Metrics



Metrics are one of the most important components in the Ravlo interface. They surface the numbers that matter — the signals investors rely on to make confident decisions.



Ravlo metrics are designed to feel:

\- precise

\- clear

\- trustworthy

\- investor‑grade

\- cinematic



1\. Purpose

Metrics highlight key values, insights, and performance indicators. They help users understand the state of a deal, portfolio, or workflow at a glance.



Metrics are used for:

\- financial KPIs

\- deal analysis

\- portfolio performance

\- risk indicators

\- progress tracking

\- summaries and snapshots



2\. Types of Metrics

Ravlo uses a disciplined set of metric types:



Primary Metric

A high‑impact number that represents the most important insight on a card or panel.



Secondary Metric

A supporting number that adds context or detail to the primary metric.



Grouped Metrics

A set of related metrics displayed together for comparison or pattern recognition.



Trend Metric

A metric paired with an up/down indicator or percentage change.



Status Metric

A metric that communicates state, such as “Pass,” “Review,” or “Risk.”



3\. Anatomy

A Ravlo metric includes:

\- label text

\- primary value

\- optional secondary value

\- optional trend or status indicator

\- spacing based on tokens

\- alignment based on grid rules



4\. Behavior

Metrics must:

\- be instantly scannable

\- use consistent formatting for numbers, currency, and percentages

\- avoid unnecessary decoration

\- maintain clear hierarchy between label and value

\- support responsive layouts



5\. Design Principles

Metrics follow Ravlo’s core principles:

\- Blueprint alignment: clean, predictable structure

\- Cinematic spacing: breathing room around key numbers

\- Clarity over cleverness: no visual noise

\- Emotional safety: stable, trustworthy presentation



Metrics are the heartbeat of the Ravlo OS. They turn raw data into clear, confident insight for investors.
