Modals



Modals are focused interaction surfaces that appear above the main interface. They are used for important decisions, confirmations, previews, and tasks that require the user’s full attention.



Ravlo modals are designed to feel:

\- intentional

\- calm

\- cinematic

\- structured

\- interruption‑worthy



1\. Purpose

Modals isolate a single task or decision. They prevent distraction and guide the user through a clear, contained moment of action.



Modals are used for:

\- confirmations

\- form submissions

\- previews

\- warnings

\- signatures

\- uploads

\- focused editing



2\. Types of Modals

Ravlo uses a disciplined set of modal types:



Standard Modal

A general-purpose modal for forms, confirmations, and simple interactions.



Large Modal

Used for content that requires more space, such as previews or multi‑section forms.



Full‑Screen Modal

Used for immersive tasks like photo viewers, map previews, or signature capture.



Side Modal

A slide‑in modal used for quick edits or lightweight workflows.



Confirmation Modal

A small modal used for yes/no or accept/decline decisions.



3\. Anatomy

A Ravlo modal includes:

\- overlay backdrop

\- container surface

\- header with title or action label

\- body content area

\- footer with actions

\- spacing based on tokens

\- radius based on radii tokens

\- shadow or glow based on elevation tokens



4\. Behavior

Modals must:

\- focus the user on a single task

\- dim the background without hiding context

\- prevent accidental dismissal during critical actions

\- support keyboard navigation and screen readers

\- scale gracefully on mobile

\- avoid overcrowding



5\. Design Principles

Modals follow Ravlo’s core principles:

\- Blueprint alignment: clean, predictable structure

\- Cinematic spacing: generous breathing room

\- Clarity over cleverness: simple, direct content

\- Emotional safety: calm, stable presentation



Modals create intentional moments in the Ravlo OS. They bring clarity, focus, and confidence to important decisions.
