Panels



Panels are one of the most important structural components in the Ravlo interface. They create defined spaces for workflows, forms, data entry, summaries, and multi‑step processes across the Ravlo OS.



Panels are designed to feel:

\- stable

\- grounded

\- architectural

\- cinematic

\- trustworthy



1\. Purpose

Panels provide a clear container for focused work. They separate tasks, guide attention, and create structure within dashboards and modules.



Panels are used for:

\- forms

\- workflows

\- detail views

\- multi‑section content

\- data review

\- next‑step actions

\- summaries and overviews



2\. Types of Panels

Ravlo uses a disciplined set of panel types:



Standard Panel

A general-purpose container for structured content.



Section Panel

Used to divide larger workflows into clear, labeled sections.



Side Panel

A slide‑in or fixed panel used for editing, reviewing, or quick actions.



Workflow Panel

A multi‑step panel used for guided processes such as deal analysis or onboarding.



Summary Panel

A panel that highlights key information, totals, or next steps.



3\. Anatomy

A Ravlo panel includes:

\- container surface

\- header with title or section label

\- body content area

\- optional footer for actions

\- spacing based on tokens

\- radius based on radii tokens

\- shadow or glow based on elevation tokens



4\. Behavior

Panels must:

\- maintain consistent padding and spacing

\- align to the grid

\- support responsive resizing

\- avoid overcrowding

\- clearly separate header, content, and actions

\- remain visually calm even with dense information



5\. Design Principles

Panels follow Ravlo’s core principles:

\- Blueprint alignment: strong grid structure

\- Cinematic spacing: generous breathing room

\- Clarity over cleverness: simple, readable layouts

\- Emotional safety: stable, grounded surfaces



Panels are the backbone of Ravlo’s workflows. They create clarity, structure, and confidence for investors navigating complex decisions.
