Badges



Badges are small visual indicators used to communicate status, category, state, or metadata. They help users quickly understand context without reading full text or scanning dense information.



Ravlo badges are designed to feel:

\- clear

\- compact

\- intentional

\- informative

\- cinematic



1\. Purpose

Badges provide quick, at‑a‑glance information. They reduce cognitive load and help users identify important details instantly.



Badges are used for:

\- statuses (Completed, Pending, Flagged)

\- categories (Rental, Flip, Development)

\- data sources (Zillow, MLS, Ravlo)

\- roles (Borrower, Partner, Lender)

\- tags and labels

\- priority indicators



2\. Types of Badges

Ravlo uses a disciplined set of badge types:



Status Badge

Communicates the state of an item, such as “Completed,” “Pending,” or “Flagged.”



Category Badge

Labels an item by type, such as “Rental,” “Flip,” or “Contractor.”



Source Badge

Indicates where data originated, such as “Zillow,” “MLS,” or “Ravlo.”



Tag Badge

Lightweight labels used for metadata or quick filtering.



Priority Badge

Highlights urgency or importance.



3\. Anatomy

A Ravlo badge includes:

\- label text

\- background color based on tokens

\- optional icon

\- spacing based on tokens

\- radius based on radii tokens



4\. Behavior

Badges must:

\- be readable at small sizes

\- maintain consistent padding

\- avoid long text labels

\- use color intentionally and sparingly

\- remain accessible with sufficient contrast



5\. Design Principles

Badges follow Ravlo’s core principles:

\- Blueprint alignment: consistent structure and spacing

\- Cinematic spacing: clean, breathable presentation

\- Clarity over cleverness: simple, direct labels

\- Emotional safety: calm, predictable color usage



Badges are the micro‑signals of the Ravlo OS. They bring clarity, structure, and meaning to complex information.
