Responsiveness



Responsiveness ensures Ravlo feels consistent, structured, and premium across all screen sizes. Every layout, component, and pattern must adapt gracefully without losing clarity or hierarchy.



Ravlo responsiveness is:

\- adaptive

\- structured

\- predictable

\- architectural

\- calm



1\. Purpose

Responsive rules ensure Ravlo maintains its cinematic, investor‑grade experience on any device or viewport.



Responsiveness defines:

\- breakpoints

\- grid behavior

\- layout adjustments

\- component scaling

\- spacing adaptation



2\. Breakpoints



Ravlo uses a simple, architectural breakpoint system:



\- Small (mobile): 0–599px  

\- Medium (tablet): 600–1023px  

\- Large (desktop): 1024–1439px  

\- Extra Large (wide desktop): 1440px+  



3\. Layout Behavior



Mobile  

\- single‑column layouts  

\- stacked content  

\- simplified navigation  



Tablet  

\- two‑column layouts where appropriate  

\- moderate spacing  

\- adaptive panels  



Desktop  

\- full grid usage  

\- cinematic spacing  

\- multi‑panel layouts  



Wide Desktop  

\- expanded grids  

\- increased spacing  

\- enhanced cinematic presence  



4\. Component Scaling



Components must:

\- maintain readability  

\- preserve spacing tokens  

\- avoid shrinking below usability thresholds  

\- adapt typography proportionally  



5\. Pattern Responsiveness



Dashboards  

\- collapse grids into vertical stacks  

\- preserve metric hierarchy  



Flows  

\- maintain step clarity  

\- avoid horizontal scrolling  



Marketing  

\- maintain cinematic spacing  

\- adapt imagery without distortion  



6\. Rules



Do:

\- maintain hierarchy across breakpoints  

\- preserve breathing room  

\- ensure touch targets remain accessible  



Don’t:

\- hide essential content  

\- compress components until unreadable  

\- break the architectural grid  



Responsiveness ensures Ravlo feels premium and intentional on every device.
