Writing



Writing guidelines ensure Ravlo communicates with clarity, confidence, and calm across product, marketing, and documentation.



Ravlo writing is:

\- clear

\- concise

\- structured

\- calm

\- confident

\- intentional



1\. Purpose

Writing guidelines define how information is communicated across the platform. They ensure consistency, readability, and investor‑grade clarity.



Writing defines:

\- structure

\- tone

\- grammar

\- hierarchy

\- microcopy rules



2\. Principles



Clarity  

Say only what matters.



Calm  

Avoid urgency, hype, or noise.



Precision  

Use accurate, investor‑grade language.



Structure  

Use short paragraphs, clear headers, and predictable rhythm.



3\. Microcopy



Rules:

\- be direct  

\- avoid filler  

\- use verbs that guide action  

\- keep labels short  

\- avoid ambiguity  



Examples:

\- “Save changes”  

\- “Add property”  

\- “View analysis”  



4\. Error Messages



Rules:

\- be clear  

\- be specific  

\- avoid blame  

\- offer next steps  



Example:

“Your document couldn’t be uploaded. Try again or choose a different file.”



5\. Success Messages



Rules:

\- be concise  

\- avoid celebration language  

\- reinforce clarity  



Example:

“Your request has been submitted.”



6\. Long‑Form Writing



Rules:

\- maintain hierarchy  

\- use scannable structure  

\- avoid dense blocks  

\- keep tone neutral and confident  



7\. Do’s \& Don’ts



Do:

\- write with intention  

\- use simple, strong sentences  

\- maintain calm tone  



Don’t:

\- use slang  

\- over‑explain  

\- use hype or exclamation marks  



Writing in Ravlo is a tool for clarity — calm, confident, and architecturally structured.
