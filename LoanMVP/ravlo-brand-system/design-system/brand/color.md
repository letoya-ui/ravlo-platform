Color



The Ravlo color system defines the core palette used across the entire ecosystem. It establishes a premium, architectural, dark‑mode‑first identity inspired by steel, slate, blueprint tones, and cinematic lighting.



Ravlo’s color language is:

\- architectural

\- calm

\- premium

\- disciplined

\- cinematic



1\. Purpose

The color system ensures consistency, clarity, and emotional tone across product and marketing surfaces. It supports hierarchy, accessibility, and investor‑grade decision making.



The color system defines:

\- primary palette

\- secondary palette

\- semantic colors

\- surface layers

\- gradients and glows

\- usage rules



2\. Primary Palette

The primary palette represents Ravlo’s core identity.



Primary colors include:

\- Midnight (base background)

\- Deep Slate (secondary background)

\- Steel (neutral surfaces)

\- Blueprint Blue (brand accent)

\- Soft Glow Blue (cinematic highlight)



3\. Secondary Palette

Secondary colors support accents, states, and subtle emphasis.



Secondary colors include:

\- muted blues

\- desaturated grays

\- soft neutrals

\- architectural metallics



4\. Semantic Colors

Semantic colors communicate meaning and system state.



Semantic colors include:

\- Success (green)

\- Warning (amber)

\- Error (red)

\- Info (blue)



Semantic colors must be used sparingly to maintain Ravlo’s calm, premium tone.



5\. Surface Layers

Ravlo uses layered surfaces to create depth and hierarchy.



Surface layers include:

\- Base surface (dark, matte)

\- Elevated surface (slightly lighter)

\- Panel surface (structured, architectural)

\- Card surface (subtle elevation)

\- Overlay surface (dimmed cinematic layer)



6\. Gradients \& Glows

Ravlo uses gradients and glows with discipline.



Rules:

\- gradients must be subtle and architectural

\- glows must be soft, cinematic, and never overpowering

\- highlights should guide attention, not distract



7\. Usage Rules

Color usage must:

\- maintain contrast and accessibility

\- avoid bright or saturated tones

\- reinforce calm, investor‑grade presentation

\- support hierarchy and clarity

\- remain consistent across modules and surfaces



The Ravlo color system creates a premium, cinematic foundation that shapes the entire visual identity of the platform.
