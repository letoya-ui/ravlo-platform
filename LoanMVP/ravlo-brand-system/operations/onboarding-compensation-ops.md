# Ravlo Onboarding and Compensation Operations

Owner: Ravlo Operations Lead

Primary operator: Sandra

Final approval: Letoya

## Purpose

This guide defines how Ravlo should handle onboarding, team records, compensation preparation, and daily operations support.

The goal is simple:

- every person should be routed to the right place
- every new user should know what to do next
- every team member should have clear responsibilities
- compensation information should be organized before Letoya reviews it
- nothing should live only in Letoya's head

---

## Sandra's operating role

Sandra's human title is:

**Ravlo Operations Lead**

Her technical role in the platform should remain:

**platform_admin**

Her company/team should be:

**Ravlo**

Sandra can help across Ravlo operations, but Letoya remains the final decision maker.

Sandra can prepare, organize, remind, follow up, and report.

Letoya approves major decisions, strategy, partnerships, pricing, legal matters, and compensation release.

---

## Daily responsibilities

### Morning review

Sandra should begin each day in the Operations Center.

She should review:

- new users
- access requests
- pending invites
- partner requests
- CRM messages
- lending follow-ups
- system notes
- any user who appears to be in the wrong role or team

### Onboarding

Sandra should verify:

- user name and email
- role
- company/team
- dashboard assignment
- profile completion
- welcome email status
- next-step instructions

If a person is unclear, Sandra should flag them for Letoya.

### User routing

Use these default paths:

- Letoya: Executive Mission Control
- Sandra: Operations Center
- internal Ravlo admin: Operations Center
- partner: Partner Dashboard
- loan officer: Loan Officer Dashboard
- processor: Processor Dashboard
- underwriter: Underwriter Dashboard
- investor: Investor OS
- borrower: Borrower Portal

### Compensation preparation

Sandra can prepare compensation notes, but does not finalize payments without Letoya.

Sandra should track:

- who worked
- what work was completed
- which file, task, or project the work relates to
- any commission, fee, or compensation note
- any missing information
- whether Letoya approval is needed

Sandra should never guess compensation terms. If something is unclear, she should mark it for review.

### End-of-day report

Sandra should send Letoya a short daily report:

- new users reviewed
- welcome emails sent
- requests reviewed
- CRM notes updated
- lending follow-ups checked
- partner requests reviewed
- compensation notes prepared
- items needing Letoya

---

## Boundaries

Sandra can:

- organize operations
- onboard users
- update CRM records
- review requests
- prepare compensation notes
- follow up on routine items
- support users
- escalate decisions

Sandra should not independently approve:

- major partnerships
- pricing changes
- legal or compliance decisions
- investor commitments
- loan decisions
- compensation release
- anything sensitive or unclear

---

## Future dashboard idea

The Operations Center should eventually include live queues for:

- onboarding queue
- partner request queue
- compensation review queue
- CRM follow-up queue
- lending follow-up queue
- end-of-day report generator

This allows Ravlo to grow from founder-led operations into system-led operations.
