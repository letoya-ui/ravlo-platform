# Operations Playbook Checklists

## Purpose

This playbook defines the daily and weekly operating checklists that keep Ravlo, Lending OS, and Caughman Mason Construction moving even when Letoya is not available to communicate every detail.

The goal is simple:

Every department should be able to start work without waiting on Letoya.

---

## Operating principle

Ravlo should reduce what has to live in Letoya's head.

If communication is slow, if Letoya is resting, injured, dealing with appointments, moving, or handling family/business pressure, the team should still know what to do next.

Dashboards, queues, checklists, and end-of-day reports should carry the routine work.

---

## Sandra daily operating checklist

### 1. Morning dashboard review

Sandra should start inside Ravlo.

Check:

- Operations Center
- new users
- new subscriptions
- access requests
- pending invites
- CRM messages
- construction office packages
- lending onboarding items
- anything waiting on Letoya

Outcome:

Sandra knows what needs action before asking Letoya questions.

---

### 2. Lending OS onboarding checklist

When someone licenses or subscribes to Lending OS, Sandra should make sure onboarding starts smoothly.

Check:

- subscriber name
- company name
- email and phone
- selected plan
- role or intended use
- login/access status
- welcome email status
- onboarding call needed
- Academy path assigned
- required documents or setup details
- questions from subscriber

Sandra can:

- send welcome emails
- answer basic onboarding questions
- explain where to log in
- guide users to Academy resources
- collect missing setup details
- schedule onboarding or follow-up calls
- escalate product, pricing, legal, licensing, or executive questions to Letoya

Sandra should not independently decide:

- pricing exceptions
- contract changes
- licensing/legal interpretations
- platform roadmap promises
- custom development commitments

Final business decisions stay with Letoya.

---

### 3. Lending OS daily follow-up routine

Every day, Sandra should check:

- new subscribers who have not logged in
- users who started onboarding but did not finish
- users who asked questions
- pending demos or onboarding calls
- missing information
- failed payments or billing confusion if visible
- Academy progress if available

Actions:

- send follow-up emails
- update CRM notes
- mark what is waiting on the user
- mark what is waiting on Letoya
- schedule next follow-up

---

## Construction office checklist

### 1. Bid Support Queue review

Sandra should check the Construction Office Packages queue every day.

Primary page:

- `/construction-office/packages`

Statuses to watch:

- bid_package_needed
- missing_information
- draft_bid_prepared
- jamaine_review_needed
- ready_to_send
- follow_up_needed

---

### 2. New bid handoff checklist

When Jamaine sends a construction opportunity to Sandra, Sandra should verify:

- project name
- category or trade type
- property address or location
- source or contact
- due date / bid deadline
- walk-through date if needed
- contact name
- contact email
- contact phone
- scope notes
- photos or documents
- insurance or license requirements
- missing information

If anything is missing, Sandra marks the status:

- missing_information

---

### 3. Bid package preparation checklist

Sandra can prepare the office side of the bid package.

Checklist:

- organize job details
- format proposal shell
- collect photos and files
- prepare contact record
- confirm due date
- draft follow-up email
- prepare notes for Jamaine
- flag questions for field review

When ready for Jamaine, Sandra marks:

- jamaine_review_needed

Sandra does not approve:

- final scope
- labor number
- material number
- final price
- job safety
- whether the project is profitable
- whether the project should be accepted

Those decisions stay with Jamaine and Letoya.

---

### 4. Bid status routine

Sandra should keep every opportunity in one current status.

Common status flow:

1. bid_package_needed
2. missing_information
3. draft_bid_prepared
4. jamaine_review_needed
5. ready_to_send
6. bid_submitted
7. follow_up_needed
8. won / lost / no_bid

No opportunity should sit without a next step.

---

## Jamaine field checklist

Jamaine should stay focused on field work and opportunity generation.

Daily field actions:

- search for demo, GC, repair, ironwork, and renovation opportunities
- save qualified opportunities into Ravlo
- send office-ready opportunities to Sandra
- walk sites when needed
- take photos and notes
- identify field risks
- estimate labor assumptions
- estimate material assumptions
- approve final bid details before submission
- update opportunity status before the end of the day

Jamaine is not expected to run the office side.

He is expected to keep the field side moving.

---

## Letoya executive checklist

Letoya should not have to chase every detail manually.

Daily executive review:

- new Lending OS subscribers
- onboarding items waiting on Sandra
- construction bids waiting on Sandra
- bids waiting on Jamaine
- bids ready to submit
- submitted bids
- awarded work
- active blockers
- revenue opportunities
- decisions only Letoya can make

Letoya should focus on:

- sales
- partnerships
- product direction
- executive decisions
- pricing and strategy
- team accountability

## Weekly scorecard

### Sandra weekly targets

- all new subscribers contacted
- all onboarding questions answered or escalated
- all construction handoffs reviewed
- no bid package left without a next step
- end-of-day reports completed
- CRM notes updated

### Jamaine weekly targets

- save new construction opportunities
- send qualified opportunities to Sandra
- complete site visits
- review packages waiting on him
- approve or decline bids quickly
- follow up on field relationships

### Letoya weekly targets

- review revenue movement
- review stuck items
- close strategic partnerships
- push Ravlo subscriptions
- make executive decisions quickly
- keep product priorities focused

---

## Rule for the team

If you do not know what to do next, check the dashboard first.

If the dashboard does not answer it, update the status and ask for help.

If Letoya is unavailable, keep the routine workflow moving and put the question in the end-of-day report.

---

## Core principle

Ravlo should have a productive day even when Letoya needs a recovery day.
